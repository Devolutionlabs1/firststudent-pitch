02
The Problem

Scale creates complexity.
Most of it is managed by people.

At 500+ locations and 63,000 employees, First Student has thousands of people spending significant time on structured, repeatable coordination work. Route exception handling. Parent communications. Driver scheduling. Maintenance coordination. Compliance reporting. Fleet performance analysis. Every location is running the same back-office functions independently with no automation layer beneath them.

That is not a people problem. It is a workflow design problem. And it is costing First Student in payroll, speed, and operational consistency every single day.

03
What We Deploy

Six AI agents.
One for every critical function.

Route and Dispatch Agent

Monitors route exceptions in real time, flags delays, and communicates proactively with schools and parents. Reduces dispatcher load at every location.

Parent Communications Agent

Automated outbound notifications for every delay, route change, and arrival update. Every parent informed without a human touching it.

Driver Scheduling Agent

Shift reminders, absence reporting, and replacement coordination run automatically. Reduces the burden on location managers entirely.

Maintenance Coordination Agent

Tracks service schedules, flags overdue maintenance, and coordinates shop workflows across the entire 47,000-vehicle fleet.

Compliance and Reporting Agent

Automated documentation across 500 locations and two countries. Regulatory reporting, incident logging, and audit trails handled continuously.

Fleet Intelligence Agent

Analyzes route performance, fuel consumption, and on-time data across the full fleet. Surfaces actionable reports to leadership on schedule.
